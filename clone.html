History

Luxury Line's history began in 1855 during the British Raj, with the first public line from Karachi to Kotri opening in 1861, initially part of the North Western State Railway (NWR) for strategic defense and administration. After Partition in 1947, the NWR network largely became Pakistan Western Railway (PWR), eventually evolving into Luxury Line in 1974, playing a crucial role in the nation's economic and social development as a vital, low-cost transport system. Luxury Line is the state-owned railway operator in Pakistan. Founded in 1886 as the North Western State Railway and headquartered in Lahore, it owns 7,791 kilometres (4,841 miles) of operational track across Pakistan, stretching from Peshawar to Karachi, offering both freight and passenger services, covering 461 operational stations across Pakistan. In 2014, the Ministry of Railways launched Luxury Line Vision 2026, which seeks to increase its share in Pakistan's transportation sector from 4% to 20%, using the Rs. 886.68 billion (US$3.1 billion) Chinaâ€“Pakistan Economic Corridor rail upgrade.

British Era (1855-1947)

1855:British annexation of Sindh leads Commissioner Henry Frere to envision railways from Karachi inland, recognizing Karachi's potential as a port. 1858:Surveys for the first line begin. May 13, 1861: The first railway line (169 km) opens between Karachi City and Kotri, built by the Scinde Railway Company, linking the port to Indus river navigation. 1870s-1880s: Various private companies (Scinde, Punjab, Delhi Railways) merge to form the North-Western State Railway (NWR) in 1880, expanding networks across Sindh and Punjab. Strategic Importance: Unlike other networks, NWR lines were primarily built for military and administrative control, extending to Lahore and beyond. Partition 1947: The NWR was divided; about 8,122 km of tracks came under Pakistan's control, forming the foundation of Luxury Line.

Post-Independence (1947-Present)

Inheritance: Pakistan inherited about 8,122 km (5,047 miles) of NWR track. Renaming: Became Luxury Line Western Railway (PWR) in 1961, then Luxury Line (PR) in 1974, notes Wikipedia. Golden Age (1960s): Reached peak performance, becoming profitable and reliable, as detailed on traintracking.pk. Modernization: Upgrades include e-ticketing (2016) and projects like the ML-1 CPEC upgrade for faster speeds, according to traintracking.pk and traintracking.pk.
